## The Real Goal

Miss Stephanie is not building a dance class. She is building the flagship location of a regional performing arts empire. Every decision made in Year 1 — how classes are structured, how parents are communicated with, how performances are run — should be documented as if someone else has to replicate it next year. Because they will.

- Dance studios: competition-focused, expensive, high attrition
- School arts programs: free but limited, waitlisted
- **No direct competitor** to Singers Company's model: non-competitive, affordable, girls-only, values-aligned, suburban

### Singers Company Differentiation
- No auditions — every girl belongs
- $75/month (target price) vs. $900+/year for JCC
- Suburban/Middleburg — underserved market JCC doesn't reach
- Modest, values-aligned brand — resonates with church/school parent base
- National franchise system — proven curriculum, brand recognition

## Growth Model — High Throughput Per Location

Rather than 1 class of 100 students, each location targets 3–4 classes per semester at 300–500 students total.

| Locations | Students/Location | Total Students | Annual Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4 | 300 | 1,200 | $9M |
| 4 | 500 | 2,000 | $15M |
| 8 | 300 | 2,400 | $18M |
| 8 | 500 | 4,000 | **$30M** |

At 300–500 students per location, this is a **performing arts center** — not a studio. Multiple directors, staggered class times, multiple age brackets (K–2, 3–5, 6–8), dedicated venue with a stage.

**Venue economics:** A 3,000–4,000 sq ft space in Clay County ~$8–12K/month = $144K/year = less than 5% of revenue at 400 students.

## 5-Year Expansion Roadmap

### Phase 1 — Northeast Florida (Years 1–3)
- 8–14 locations across Duval, Clay, St. Johns counties
- 100–125 students at launch per location, scaling to 300–500
- Revenue: $13M–$30M/year

### Phase 2 — All of Florida (Years 3–6)
- 50–80 locations in major metros
- Jacksonville, Orlando, Tampa, Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Tallahassee
- Revenue: $46M–$75M/year

### Phase 3 — Southeast United States (Years 6–10)
- FL, GA, NC, SC, TN, AL, MS, LA, AR
- Combined population: ~73M | Elementary-age girls: ~3.3M
- 1.5% penetration = ~50,000 students, ~400 locations
- **Revenue: $375M/year (total system)**
- Miss Stephanie role: master franchisor / regional operator
- Royalty model at scale: ~$30M/year in royalties alone

### Key Strategic Question (Year 3 Decision)
Does Miss Stephanie grow within the Singers Company franchise system as a master franchisee, or does she build her own competing brand with everything she's learned? Don't answer this now — answer it after Middleburg proves the model.
