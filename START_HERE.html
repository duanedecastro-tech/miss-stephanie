One-Time Setup

Do this once before you start. Takes 5 minutes.

Before you begin scheduling posts, make sure both of these are set up in your browser. You only need to do this once.

  • ✓
    Sign into Google. Open your browser and make sure you're logged into the Google account that owns the Spring Photos Drive folder. The post photos load directly from Google Drive — if you're not signed in, the photos won't show up.
  • ✓
    Sign into Meta Business Suite. Go to business.facebook.com and log into your account. This is where you'll schedule all your Instagram and Facebook posts at the same time.
💡

You can have both tabs open at the same time — Google Drive in one tab, Meta Business Suite in another. That makes dragging photos faster.

Open the Calendar

This tells you which post to schedule, when, and which photo to use.

Open calendar.md — you can open it in any text editor (Notepad works fine) or in a browser. This file is your master schedule. Every post is listed with:

  • →
    The post number (e.g., Post #6)
  • →
    The date and day of the week to post it
  • →
    The best time to schedule it
  • →
    The photo file name to use (e.g., IMG10906)

Work through the calendar from top to bottom. Each session, pick the next 3–5 posts you want to schedule and do them all at once.

3

How to Schedule One Post

Repeat this for all 29 posts. Takes about 3 minutes per post.

Here is the exact workflow for scheduling a single post. Once you do it two or three times, it becomes automatic.

  • 1
    Open the post file. Double-click post_XX.html — it opens in your browser. Scroll to the bottom section labeled "Caption — Copy/Paste Ready."
  • 2
    Copy the caption. Click and drag to select all the caption text, then copy it (Ctrl+C on Windows, Cmd+C on Mac). The hashtags are already included at the bottom of that box.
  • 3
    Find the photo in Google Drive. Open your Spring Photos folder in Google Drive. The photo name is listed in the purple box at the bottom of each post file (e.g., Photo: IMG10906). Find that file in Drive.
  • 4
    Go to Meta Business Suite. Click Create Post. Select both Instagram and Facebook at the same time — one post, both platforms.
  • 5
    Add the photo. Drag the photo from your Google Drive folder directly into the Meta post. Or click the photo upload button and select it.
  • 6
    Paste the caption. Click in the caption field and paste (Ctrl+V). Everything — the caption text and all hashtags — is already there.
  • 7
    Set the date and time. Instead of clicking "Post Now," click "Schedule." Set the date and time from the purple box at the bottom of the post file, or from calendar.md.
  • 8
    Click Schedule. Done. Move to the next post in the calendar.

Where do I find the caption + hashtags? Scroll to the bottom section of any post file labeled "Caption — Copy/Paste Ready." Everything you need to copy is right there in that one box.

Where do I find the date and time? Look for the purple strip at the very bottom of each post file. It says: Schedule: [date] · Best time: [time] · Photo: [IMG name]

The Two Special Posts

Posts #5 and #17 have no photos. Here's what to do instead.

Two posts in the campaign use a custom graphic card that's built right into the HTML file — no separate photo needed. Instead of uploading a photo from Drive, you'll take a screenshot of the HTML page and upload that screenshot.

  • 1
    Open post_05.html or post_17.html in your browser.
  • 2
    Take a screenshot of the graphic card at the top of the page. On Windows: press Windows + Shift + S and drag to select just the graphic. On Mac: press Cmd + Shift + 4.
  • 3
    Upload that screenshot as the photo in Meta Business Suite. Then paste the caption as normal.

Pro Tips for Best Results

Small things that make a real difference.
  • ✓
    Schedule in batches. Sit down once a week and schedule the next 3 posts all at once. Takes about 10 minutes. Then you don't have to think about it again until next week.
  • ✓
    Check comments the day after each post. If someone comments "How do I sign up?" or "Is this still open?", reply quickly. Fast replies boost reach on both platforms.
  • ✓
    Share to your personal account too. After each post goes live on the Singers Company page, share it to your personal Facebook and Instagram as well. Your personal followers are your warmest audience.
  • ✓
    On Post #15 (Jun 10), actually tag a mom. That post says "tag a mom you know." When you post it, go into the comments yourself and tag 2–3 specific moms from Middleburg or Clay County. That jumpstarts the tagging chain.
  • ✓
    Pin Post #17 to the top of your Facebook page. After the enrollment-open post goes live on June 15, pin it to the top of your Facebook page so it's the first thing anyone sees when they visit.
